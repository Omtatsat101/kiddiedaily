About KiddieDaily

A small daily news site for families, with research-backed fact checks. Built by parents, free to read, no accounts required to use the Kid Zone.

What we're trying to do

The information environment most families live in is chaotic. Headlines optimize for emotion. Algorithms push the most engaging take rather than the most accurate one. Public figures repeat claims that sound scary or hopeful but don't survive a quick check against the primary sources.

KiddieDaily is a small attempt to do something different for the news that ends up in a family's day. We curate stories from credible sources, summarize them honestly, and attach a fact check to claims that need one. Kid Zone is the default — what you see when you arrive — and it's filtered for age-appropriateness. Parent Zone (coming soon) unlocks the full feature set.

Who runs this

KiddieDaily is one of several small projects we publish under Legacy Bridge Alliance Group, our family holding company. Sister sites include KiddieWordle (free word games), KiddieSketch (custom sketch gifts), and KiddieGo (outdoor and active kids gear).

The same family runs all of them. The same values run through all of them.

Editorial standards

Every news article on KiddieDaily follows these rules:

  • Sources are cited. Every claim of fact links to a primary source — a peer-reviewed paper, a government report, an established wire service (AP, Reuters), or an on-the-record statement.
  • AI assists, humans approve. We use AI to help draft, summarize, and identify sources. A human editor reviews and approves every published article. AI never publishes directly to the live site.
  • Fact checks have status banners. Verified, mixed evidence, false, or opinion — clearly labeled at the top.
  • Mistakes get corrections. If we get something wrong and a reader points it out, we update the article with a clear correction note. We don't delete the original to hide the change.
  • Kid Zone is age-appropriate. We skip graphic violence, sexual content, and anything that frames addiction, self-harm, or dangerous stunts approvingly. Kid Zone news is written at a 4th-6th grade reading level.

Our principles

  1. If a child can read it, it must be true. Kid-facing content is held to the same factual standard as adult-facing content. We don't simplify by stripping out important nuance.
  2. Sources, not vibes. Opinions are labeled as opinions. Claims of fact must be checked.
  3. No dark patterns. No notifications pestering you to come back. No streaks pressuring daily visits. No accounts required for the default Kid Zone experience.
  4. Privacy by default. We don't run third-party trackers. The only third-party code on the site is AdSense, when ads are enabled, and our privacy page documents exactly what that involves.
  5. Honest about what we are. KiddieDaily is a small operation. We're not The New York Times. We do this carefully but we have a small staff. When we're not sure about something, we say so.

How we make money

For now, ads (Google AdSense). The Kid Zone uses non-personalized, contextual ads only, as required for child-directed content. The Parent Zone, when it launches, will use standard personalized ads for adult readers who choose to unlock it.

Future revenue may include voluntary subscriptions and clearly-labeled sponsored features. We will never make content paywalled in a way that hides facts from families who can't pay.

Corrections, story tips, partnership inquiries: contact us. We read every message.
