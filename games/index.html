Critical Thinking

🎯 Spot the Bias Challenge

Every article on KiddieDaily shows where the outlet leans on the political spectrum. But can you spot bias in the headline itself — before you read the story?

Try this: Read two headlines on the same topic from different outlets. Look for:

  • Emotional or charged words ("slams", "blasts", "soars", "fails")
  • Whose perspective is front and center
  • What facts are left out of the headline
  • Whether the headline matches the actual story

Google Fact Check Explorer Snopes Science News NASA.gov

If you can verify it in under 3 minutes — you just fact-checked a story. That's what journalists do all day.

Science Skills

🔬 Science or Spin?

Science stories have a different structure than political stories. In a good science story, you should be able to find:

  • A named researcher or institution
  • Where the study was published (journal name)
  • How many subjects were studied
  • What the researchers actually measured
  • A caveat — what the study did NOT prove

Pick a science article from KiddieDaily and see how many of these 5 you can find. If you can find all 5, it's a solid study. If you find fewer than 3, be skeptical.
